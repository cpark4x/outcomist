## Tier 1 Response

You're framing this as "stable money ($6K/month client) vs bet on product ($2K MRR)" - but that's not the real question.

**The real question is: "Am I choosing safety because I'm afraid to commit to the thing I actually want?"**

Your product went from $500 → $2,000 MRR in 6 months (4X growth), 97% retention. That's CRUSHING IT. But you're considering $40/hour client work because "market is tough." The scarcity is in your head, not your bank account.

## Discovery Session

### Round 1: Client Opportunity Details

**Q1**: Tell me about this client opportunity - why now? What's driving their urgency?

**A1**: "it's a relatively new customer. they pitched me with the idea and i think they are looking for cost control. $6k for 35 hours is very light. but the market is really tough and I'm thankful for th ework."

**Key Insight Revealed**:
- $6K for 35 hours/week = **$40/hour** (very low freelance rate)
- Client is "looking for cost control" = wants cheap labor
- User's language: "I'm thankful for the work" = scarcity mindset signal
- Contradiction: "Market is tough" but product grew 4X in 6 months

### Round 2: Financial Runway

**Q2**: Let's talk runway - what's your monthly burn, how much savings do you have, and how long can you afford to say no to $6K? Any financial dependents or obligations that constrain the decision?

**A2**: "monthly burn is about 5k / 60k in savings / 12-14 months without new income / none / my girlfriend"

**Critical Context Revealed**:
- **12-14 months runway** with $60K savings
- Monthly burn: $5K (manageable)
- No dependents or obligations constraining decision
- **The scarcity is in the HEAD, not in the bank account**
- Client only buys 1.2 months of runway ($6K once)

### Round 3: Product Metrics

**Q3**: Product metrics - what was MRR 6 months ago, what's the growth rate, how many paying users, and what's your churn rate?

**A3**: "MRR was 500k 6 months ago / it is growing about 100 per month / 200 paying 10 per month / 3% chrun"

**Fundamentals Are Crushing It**:
- MRR growth: $500 → $2,000 in 6 months = **4X growth**
- Growth rate: $100/month (steady, not slowing)
- Paying users: 200 users at $10/month
- Retention: 97% (3% churn is excellent)
- **Product is at inflection point** - this is GO TIME, not pause time

## Recommendation Provided

**Clear Stance**: Say No to Client, Go ALL IN on Product

### Why This Recommendation:

1. **The Math Strongly Favors the Product**
   - Client: $6K/month = $40/hour (below market rate for 35 hours/week)
   - Product: $2K MRR growing $100/month = potential $5K+ MRR in 6 months
   - Compounding: Product revenue compounds, client work is linear
   - Taking client = pausing product at its **inflection point** (4X growth)

2. **You Have 12-14 Months Runway - The Scarcity is in Your Head**
   - $60K savings / $5K burn = **you can afford to bet on yourself**
   - Client only buys 1.2 months of runway ($6K / $5K burn)
   - Product could be $5K+ MRR (covering burn) in 6 months without client
   - **Fear-based decision, not math-based decision**

3. **This Client Opportunity is Actually a RED FLAG**
   - $40/hour is **well below market** for experienced freelancers
   - Client pitched YOU (not the other way around) - suggests desperation
   - "Cost control" framing = they want cheap labor
   - "Market is tough" mindset = operating from scarcity, not opportunity

4. **Your Product is at the Inflection Point - Pausing Now = Death**
   - 4X growth in 6 months = momentum
   - 97% retention = product-market fit
   - 200 paying users = real traction, not experiments
   - Pausing for 6 months = losing momentum, competitors catch up, users churn

### Next Actions:
1. Email client TODAY: "Thank you, but I'm focusing full-time on my product"
2. Audit your spending (this week): Can you reduce $5K/month burn to $4K or $3.5K?
3. Double down on growth (next 3 months): What got you from $500 → $2,000 MRR?
4. Set revenue milestone (90 days): Hit $3K MRR by February 2026
5. Revisit client work ONLY if: Product growth stalls OR runway drops below 6 months

## Pattern Captured - Scarcity Mindset Hiding Opportunity

This test led to documenting Pattern #6 in the pattern library.

**Surface request**: "Should I take stable money or bet on risky thing?"

**Signals to recognize**:
- Fear-based language ("market is tough," "I'm thankful," "playing it safe")
- Strong fundamentals contradicting scarcity narrative (runway, growth, retention)
- Underpriced "stable" option (below-market rates framed as opportunity)
- Inflection point timing (growth momentum that would be lost if paused)
- Framing choice as "safety vs risk" when math shows risk is minimal

**Real problem**: Fear preventing commitment to opportunity despite having resources and validation

**Key questions to ask**:
1. What's your actual financial runway? (reveal false scarcity)
2. What are the fundamentals of what you're "risking"? (reveal strength)
3. What's the hourly rate of the "stable" option? (reveal if underpriced)
4. What happens if you pause momentum? (reveal opportunity cost)

---

## What Could Improve

### ⚠️ Could Have Asked About Product Pause Impact Earlier

**Current question flow:**
1. Client opportunity details
2. Financial runway
3. Product metrics

**Better flow:**
1. Product metrics first (understand what's being paused)
2. Client opportunity details (compare rates/value)
3. Financial runway (do you need this?)
4. What happens to product if you pause 6 months? (real cost)

**Why**: Asking about product metrics FIRST would have revealed strong fundamentals immediately, reframing the entire decision from "can I afford risk?" to "can I afford to pause?"

### ⚠️ Didn't Probe "Market is Tough" Mindset Early Enough

**What happened**: User said "market is really tough" in Q1, but didn't challenge until recommendation

**Better**: When user says "market is tough" in Q1, immediately ask: "What makes you say the market is tough? Your product grew 4X - that suggests the market is working for you."

### ⚠️ Could Have Explored Why $40/Hour Rate Was Acceptable

**Missing question**:
- "You're an experienced freelancer with a product at $2K MRR - why is $40/hour acceptable to you?"
- "What's your usual freelance rate? How does $40/hour compare?"
- "Have you considered raising your rate to $80-100/hour if you need short-term income?"

**Why it matters**: Might reveal deeper self-worth or confidence issues beneath the scarcity mindset.
